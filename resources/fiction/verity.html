Verity

Verity — Full Spoiler Summary

The Setup

Lowen Ashleigh is a struggling writer. One day she witnesses a gruesome accident in New York where a pedestrian is killed by a truck. A man named Jeremy Crawford helps her through the shock.

Later she discovers that Jeremy is the husband of famous bestselling author Verity Crawford. Verity has supposedly been left unable to communicate after a serious car accident. Since Verity can no longer finish her bestselling series, Jeremy hires Lowen to complete the remaining books. So Lowen moves into the Crawford family mansion to go through Verity's notes.

The Family Tragedy

Before Lowen arrives, the Crawford family has suffered multiple disasters. Jeremy and Verity had twin daughters: Chastin and Harper. One twin died in what was believed to be a tragic accident. Later the other twin also died. Then Verity herself was injured in a car accident and became almost completely incapacitated. To outsiders, the family seems cursed.

The Manuscript

While searching Verity's office, Lowen finds a hidden manuscript. This is where the novel becomes disturbing. The manuscript appears to be Verity's autobiography. As Lowen reads it, she becomes horrified.

What the Manuscript Says

  • She never wanted children — Verity admits she was obsessed with Jeremy but never wanted to become a mother. She claims she became pregnant mainly because Jeremy wanted children.
  • She was jealous of her daughters — After the twins were born, Verity describes intense resentment. She hated how Jeremy's attention shifted toward the children.
  • Chastin was Jeremy's favorite — Verity becomes especially jealous because Jeremy appears to prefer Chastin.
  • She imagines harming her children — The manuscript contains numerous thoughts about hurting the twins.

The Staircase Incident

One of the most shocking revelations: Verity allegedly intentionally pushes one of the twins down a staircase. The child survives. Everyone assumes it was an accident. Verity secretly knows it wasn't.

Attempted Drowning

The manuscript claims Verity later tries to drown Harper. Again, she gets away with it because nobody realizes what happened.

Chastin's Death

Eventually Chastin dies. The official story is an accident. But the manuscript strongly suggests Verity intentionally caused it. This becomes one of the book's biggest horrors.

Lowen Becomes Obsessed

While reading all this, Lowen becomes terrified. She begins believing Verity is a hidden psychopath. At the same time she starts falling in love with Jeremy. This creates a very uncomfortable situation: Jeremy's wife is still alive, Lowen is secretly reading private confessions, and Lowen is becoming emotionally involved with Jeremy.

Strange Things Start Happening

Lowen begins noticing things that shouldn't happen. Verity appears more aware than she should be. Eyes seem to follow her. Verity appears to move. Verity seems to react when no one is watching. Lowen starts wondering: "What if Verity is only pretending to be disabled?"

The Big Reveal

Eventually Lowen becomes convinced that Verity is faking her condition. One day she catches evidence suggesting Verity can move and understand everything. Now Lowen believes Verity is dangerous, killed at least one child, and has been pretending for years.

The Confrontation

Lowen and Jeremy discover the manuscript. Jeremy reads the horrifying confessions. He becomes convinced Verity murdered their daughter. Enraged, Jeremy confronts Verity. The confrontation ends with Jeremy killing Verity. At this point readers think the mystery is solved.

The Twist

After Verity dies, Lowen finds a hidden letter. And this changes everything.

What the Letter Says

The letter claims the manuscript was never a confession. According to Verity, she was writing an exercise called "antagonistic journaling" — she was intentionally writing the worst possible version of herself. None of it was real. The manuscript was fiction.

She claims she loved her children, never harmed them, the incidents were accidents, and Jeremy misunderstood everything. She also claims she pretended to remain disabled because she feared Jeremy would kill her if he read the manuscript.

The Final Question

The novel never gives a definitive answer. Readers still argue about it years later.

Was the manuscript true? If yes, Verity was a murderous psychopath and Jeremy killed the right person.

Was the letter true? If yes, Verity was innocent, Jeremy murdered his wife, and Lowen helped cover it up.

Many believe the manuscript was the truth and the letter was a desperate lie. Others believe the letter was true and the manuscript was merely a writing exercise. The entire reputation of Verity largely comes from this ambiguity.

If Someone Asks You About Verity

A good 30-second explanation would be:

"It's a psychological thriller about a writer named Lowen who is hired to finish a famous author's books. She discovers a secret manuscript that seems to reveal the author, Verity, is a child murderer and psychopath. After Verity dies, a letter is found claiming the manuscript was fiction. The whole novel revolves around deciding which version of the story is true."

That's essentially the core of Verity and the reason people keep talking about it.
